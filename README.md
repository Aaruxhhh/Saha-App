# Saha (साहा) — because we all need a hand sometimes.  #
## What is this?  ##

I built Saha (which basically means "together" or "with" in Sanskrit) because the way we help each
other in our neighborhoods is kind of broken.

Right now, if an Uncle in Sector 119 needs help setting up a new router or a kid is stuck on a math
problem, they usually just wait for their family or struggle through it. Meanwhile, there are plenty
of students nearby who could use a quick ₹200-₹500 side hustle.

Saha is a hyper-local marketplace that connects those two people. It's like TaskRabbit, but with
a soul and a much better-looking dark mode.
## Why I'm building this ##

  ### The "Parents Left Behind" problem: ### 
  A lot of us are moving away for work or studies
    (shoutout to everyone heading to China or Ireland). Our parents are tech-savvy, but they
    shouldn't have to wait for a weekend phone call to fix a Windows error.
    The Student Hustle: Being a student in India is expensive. If you’re good at Math, Tech, or
    just don't mind carrying groceries, you should be able to earn while helping your
    community.
    Hyper-Local over Global: I don't care about "scaling to 100 cities." I care about making it
    work perfectly in one society (like Gaur Grandeur) first.

## The Vibe (The Tech Stuff) ##

I’m a big fan of the Apple aesthetic , so I spent way too much time making this look like a native
iOS app.

  ### The Look: ###
  Dark mode , glassmorphism , and neon glows. It looks more like Apple Music than
    a "service" app.
    
  ### The Safety: ###
  Everything runs on an Escrow model. The money is locked safely when you
    book, and it's only released when the job is done (via a PIN/QR handshake).
    
  ### The Verified Badge: ### 
  Every helper goes through a proper KYC/Aadhaar check because trust
    is everything when you're entering someone's home.

## How it works (The ₹200 Example) ##

    Uncle needs help: He posts a job (e.g., "Install Windows") for ₹ 200.
    Saha holds the cash: Uncle pays ₹200 via UPI. It sits in the app's "vault."
    The Job: A student nearby sees the notification, walks over, and fixes it.
    The Payout: Uncle gives the student a PIN. The student gets ₹ 170 , and I keep ₹ 30 to keep the servers (and my coffee habit) running.
